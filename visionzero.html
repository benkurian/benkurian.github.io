Executive Summary

Every year, nearly 43,000 lives are lost to traffic crashes in the United States, with millions more injured. This crisis isn't just a headline—it’s deeply personal, impacting families, friends, and communities. Achieving Vision Zero, the goal of eliminating all roadway fatalities, demands bold action and transformative change.

For decades, road safety has relied on a reactive approach, addressing danger only after lives have been lost. The Safe System framework, endorsed by NHTSA, shifts the focus to proactive strategies. By emphasizing safer people, safer roads, safer vehicles, safer speeds, and post-crash care, it acknowledges that human error is inevitable but doesn't have to result in fatalities.

Artificial intelligence builds on this framework, offering unparalleled opportunities to analyze data and act swiftly. AI can monitor driving behaviors, identify hazardous conditions, and optimize traffic flow in real time. It enhances vehicle safety through advanced systems like auto-braking and lane-assist and supports faster emergency response through predictive analytics. This technology doesn’t replace human responsibility—it complements it, providing tools to prevent crashes and save lives.

However, significant challenges remain. AI systems rely on high-quality, unbiased data, yet issues like incomplete data and privacy concerns undermine their potential. Legal uncertainties and public skepticism, particularly around autonomous vehicles, pose barriers to adoption. Addressing these challenges requires collaboration across governments, industry, and communities, alongside measures like balanced datasets, transparent AI processes, and stronger privacy protections.

Achieving Vision Zero also demands a cultural shift. Road safety isn't just about technology—it’s about changing behaviors and fostering accountability among all road users. Together, the integration of AI into the Safe System framework and a commitment to responsible driving decisions will create a safer environment for everyone.

We are at a crossroads. The cost of inaction isn’t just measured in dollars, but in human lives. The choices made today—embracing innovation, supporting policy change, and valuing safety—will determine whether Vision Zero becomes a reality. It starts and ends with us.
